Emergency Support Function 8

Core ESF-8 functions.

Emergency Support Function 8 organizes public health and medical support during disasters and emergencies. These 17 functions define the major areas of ESF-8 responsibility across assessment, medical response, safety, environmental health, and consequence management.

Explore Resources Back to Home
17

Core public health and medical response functions grouped into five operational categories.

Assessment Medical Response Safety Environment Consequence Management

Assessment & Surveillance

How ESF-8 identifies needs, tracks health threats, and supports decision-making.

4 functions
01

Assessment of public health & medical needs

Identifies health and medical gaps, affected populations, facility needs, and priority response requirements.

02

Health surveillance

Monitors health indicators, disease activity, injury patterns, and emerging threats during incidents.

03

All-hazards public health & medical consultation, technical assistance & support

Provides subject-matter expertise to responders, agencies, healthcare systems, and partner organizations.

04

Public health & medical information

Supports accurate, timely, and coordinated health information for public and operational audiences.

Medical Response

Clinical, healthcare, behavioral health, veterinary, and logistical support during emergencies.

6 functions
05

Medical surge

Supports expanded healthcare capacity when patient volume exceeds routine operations.

06

Health, medical & veterinary equipment and supplies

Coordinates critical equipment, medical supplies, pharmaceuticals, and veterinary response resources.

07

Patient movement

Supports coordinated movement of patients when facilities, regions, or systems are overwhelmed.

08

Patient care

Supports direct medical care, clinical coordination, and continuity of healthcare services.

09

Behavioral healthcare

Addresses mental health, stress, trauma, and behavioral health needs of affected populations and responders.

10

Veterinary medical support

Supports animal health, service animals, livestock concerns, and veterinary public health needs.

Safety & Security

Protection of medical products, food systems, agriculture, blood, and tissues.

4 functions
11

Safety & security of drugs, biologics & medical devices

Protects the integrity, safety, and availability of medical products during emergencies.

12

Blood and tissues

Supports the safety, supply, and coordination of blood products and tissue resources.

13

Food safety and defense

Addresses contamination, disruption, and intentional or accidental threats to the food supply.

14

Agriculture safety and security

Supports agricultural health, security, and protection of related systems and resources.

Public Health & Environment

Environmental health supports that reduce secondary public health impacts after an incident.

2 functions
15

Vector control

Supports monitoring and control of insects or animals that can spread disease after emergencies.

16

Guidance on potable water, wastewater & solid waste disposal

Provides health guidance for safe water, sanitation, waste handling, and related environmental risks.

Consequence Management

Specialized support for the most severe health and medical consequences.

1 functions
17

Mass fatality management, victim identification & mitigating health hazards from contaminated remains

Supports respectful fatality management while reducing health hazards and supporting identification efforts.

Use these functions as a response framework.

The ESF-8 functions can guide planning, training, exercises, and resource organization across public health, healthcare, emergency management, and academic partners.
